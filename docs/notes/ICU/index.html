    diagnosing ARDS

    • related: ARDS, ICU
    • tags: #icu

    Diagnosis and definition for ARDS

    • ARDS is diagnosed as acute onset (less than a week) of bilateral noncardiogenic pulmonary edema in setting of PEEP.
    • Mild, moderate, severe ARDS are defined by PaO2 to FiO2 ratio
      • P/F 200<300 is mild
      • P/F 100<200 is moderate and sometimes called moderately severe
      • P/F <100 is severe

    icu intro lecture hypoxia

    • related: ICU
    • tags: #icu

    causes of hypoxemia

    • Low FiO2 or PiO2
    • Hypoventilation
      • apneic patient
    • Diffusion gradient
      • pulmonary edema, ARDS, fibrosis
    • Dead space: ventilated but not perfused. V/Q = infinity
    • Shunt: perfused but not ventilated. V/Q = 0

    Shunt

    • causes of shunt: anything that causes collapsed alveoli
      • atelectasis
      • ARDS
      • surfacta

  • Ventilation Strategies for ARDS Includes Low Tidal Volume, High Peep, Target Plateau Pressure of 30

    ventilation strategies for ARDS includes low tidal volume, high peep, target plateau pressure of 30

    • related: ARDS, ICU
    • tags: #icu #permanent

    Notes

    Ventilation strategies for ARDS includes PEEP, tidal volume, and plateau pressure.

    • Use low tidal volume, use 4 to 8 cc/kg ideal body weight. Target 6 cc/kg
      • make sure to measure patients as females under report their height and males overreport
    • Target plateau pressure < 30 cm H2O
    • Use PEEP table
