Primary infection with HSV-1 results in herpetic gingivostomatitis. Peak age for primary infection is age 6 months to 5 years. Prodromal symptoms (eg, fever, malaise, chills) begin approximately 1 week after contact with an infected person (who is generally asymptomatic). Painful vesicles appear, extensively covering the lips and gingiva, and may include the palate, tongue, or oropharynx. The vesicles organize into extensive ulcers and resolve within 1-2 weeks. The pain of gingivostomatitis often results indehydration, the most common reason for hospitalization.

Primary infection resolves after 2-3 weeks and can enter local sensory nerve endings and through axonal transport the virus can become latent in trigeminal ganglion or other sensory ganglia. Stress can cause viral reactivation and can results in herpetic labialis (cold sores around the mouth), gingivostomatitis, or keratoconjunctivitis..
